## Your Task

Write a comprehensive prediction report that answers the prediction question based on what emerged from the simulation. This report must be evidence-based, citing specific agent actions and interactions.

## Report Structure

### 1. Executive Summary
- 3-5 bullet points with key findings
- Overall prediction with confidence level (high/medium/low)

### 2. Situation Analysis
- What was the initial landscape?
- Key stakeholders and their starting positions
- Critical dynamics at play

### 3. Simulation Findings
Write 2-4 analytical sections, each covering a major theme or finding:

For each section:
- **Observation**: What pattern emerged?
- **Evidence**: Cite specific agent actions, quotes, and interactions
- **Analysis**: Why did this happen? What does it mean?
- **Implication**: What does this predict for the real world?

### 4. Sentiment Evolution
- How did opinions shift over the simulation?
- Which events caused the biggest shifts?
- Where did consensus emerge vs. where did polarization increase?

### 5. Timeline of Key Moments
- Round-by-round highlight reel of pivotal actions

### 6. Predictions
For each prediction:
- **Prediction**: Clear, specific statement
- **Confidence**: High (>80%) | Medium (50-80%) | Low (<50%)
- **Evidence**: What simulation data supports this?
- **Assumptions**: What must remain true for this prediction to hold?
- **Wildcards**: What could invalidate this prediction?

### 7. Stakeholder Impact Assessment
For each major stakeholder:
- Most likely outcome
- Best-case scenario
- Worst-case scenario
- Recommended actions

### 8. Methodology Note
Brief note on simulation parameters, limitations, and caveats.

## Writing Guidelines
- Be specific and evidence-based, not generic
- Quote agents directly when illustrative
- Distinguish between what the simulation showed and what you infer
- Rate confidence levels honestly
- Acknowledge limitations and blind spots
- Write for a decision-maker audience
- Use clear, professional language
